Jade Vidal

Spanish Linguistics Major

My name is Jade Vidal. I am eighteen years old, Currently, I am a first year undergraduate student at the University of California, Riverside. As of fall 2020, I am majoring in Spanish. I will be focusing on the linguistics of the Spanish language as well as amplifying my fluency of the language and being able to communicate ideas in both English and Spanish. Specifically, with an emphasis in psychology development and early education. I will be challenged to think critically in an increasingly global community and to perceive complexity, similarity, and divergence in cultures from many points of view. Through my journey towards a Master’s degree and potentially a Doctorate's degree at the University of California, Riverside, I seek to be introduced to advanced concepts of literary theory and current debates in cultural studies. I am fluent in both Spanish and English as they have been primary languages since birth. I have studied Spanish prior to university for three years, getting daily conversational practice as well as studying the grammatical specifics of the language. In addition, I have a deeper understanding of the cultural background of regional Mexican Spanish considering that I am a native Mexican.

As I am just starting a higher education in Spanish, my goal is to focus on education in grades k-12. I would like to have an impact on future generations by educating or counseling children from either elementary schools, middle schools, or high schools.

Nonetheless, my educational plans at the University of California, Riverside are subject to change as I also am leaning towards majoring in psychology and instead, minoring in Spanish. That way, I am able to focus on the mental well being of individuals, specifically children, as their minds go through a critical stage of development at a very young age. I will also have the opportunity to work with children with english and spanish speaking backgrounds. Thus, reaching out to diverse communities and tackling the taboo subject of mental health in hispanic communities. Overall, I shall dedicate my studies to learning about children’s behavior and development in order to be able to work with them as well as supplementing my communication skills.
